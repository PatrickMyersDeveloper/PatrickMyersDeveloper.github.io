It's good to see you.

Let me tell you a story.

Like many things, this all goes back to my parents. Growing up, they had a lot of photos and photo albums floating around our Northern Michigan house. The photos were mostly cheap drugstore prints years (sometimes decades) older than me. The photo albums were much the same: old, fake leather coated in dust and basement smell. A large majority of them were captured while my parents were attending Northern Michigan University in Marquette, MI during the late 80's and early 90's. Technically the photos weren't anything special, most of them captured on the 35mm point-and-shoot style cameras that were popular at the time. I became so drawn to these photos because they painted an incredibly vivid picture of a time in my parents life they both held so dear. Most of them were hastily shot candids, which to me, is what made them so special. Rather than well-composed stills, they were more documentary.

Fast forward to the Summer between my second and third year of college, I was at a local thrift store with a friend of mine. She was browsing the shelves in the tech/electronics section when she pointed out an old camera to me. It was a black point-and-shoot film camera, like those my parents used to shoot on. Looking down on it, the word Yashica T2 popped in cherry red lettering. I didnâ€™t know it at the time, but it would become a significant object in my life. After paying the princely sum of $2.50, and special ordering a battery from Jeff Bezos, it came to life. Since that time, that camera has captured hundreds of photos, been to more than 20 states, and spent a substantial amount of time in my back pocket. It became my vessel for capturing memories the way my parents and their friends had almost 35 years later.
